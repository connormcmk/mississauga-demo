# Pricing

Canonical Louie pricing. Outreach copy, the landing page, and bid responses
should all match this file. Update here first, then update downstream.

## Headline

- **$30 per meeting** to ingest historical record (one-time backfill)
- **$9,500 / year** ongoing, all new meetings included
- For a city Mississauga's size: ~200 meetings/year of historical backfill
  ≈ **$6,000 one-time**, then **$9,500/year** thereafter

## Comparative framing

> "Won't cost a tenth of what eScribe costs."

eScribe is the incumbent agenda-management vendor in most Ontario
municipalities. Louie is a complement, not a replacement — eScribe handles
agenda packaging and meeting management; Louie sits on top of the published
record and makes it searchable for residents and staff.

## What's included

- Full ingestion of council, committee, and sub-committee video, minutes,
  agendas, and staff reports
- Public-facing search interface (no login required for residents)
- Citation-backed answers — every response links to the source line
- Speaker name and title accuracy (sourced from public records + internal
  configuration)
- Hosting and inference costs absorbed in the annual fee

## What's not included

- Negation Game / deliberation-map authoring tools (separate offering)
- Custom integrations beyond the published record
- White-label branding

## Procurement notes

- Some municipalities can route this through an unsolicited-bid process.
  Confirm with the city's purchasing office before assuming a sole-source path.
- Key open question for any prospect: **when do you expect this kind of
  functionality to become standard?** The honest answer is "soon" — that
  framing tends to land.
