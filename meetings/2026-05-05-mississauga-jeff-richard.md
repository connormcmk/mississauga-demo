# Mississauga — 2026-05-05

**Attendees**
- Us: Paul Winsor
- Them:
  - Jeff — Chief Administrative Officer
  - Richard — Strategic Communications and Initiatives Director

**Format**
20-min intro / pitch (they had to move to the next meeting at the 20-min mark).

## What happened

Jeff had never thought of making it easier for constituents to look at
the record.

Richard, on eScribe's search experience: *"Ah, the search bar takes you
here, but then you have to watch the whole thing!"* He'd already felt the
problem firsthand — getting records off the city website was slow, and
search wasn't very helpful.

They were acting in the way you would if the response was *"oh, that
makes sense."* No objection, neither for the AI component nor for
deliberative maps.

They could see this would make things easier for constituents, and they
could see the existing system is pretty impenetrable. Short meeting, so
transparency wasn't directly mentioned, but they could see the poor
citizen experience.

## Reactions and tells

Richard did ask, *"Would we just use ChatGPT for this?"* Paul explained
that the data is not in ChatGPT so it's not accessible, but even if it
were, there's so much information in ChatGPT that it would blur
everything out anyway.

Richard then stated, *"So this would be an AI that's really developed
around the Mississauga record."* He got it in the end.

Distinguishing they-liked-it from they-understood-it: they understood it
and didn't push back. We don't yet have a strong signal on internal
champions or budget authority.

## Pricing / commercial signals

Paul mentioned price *"remarkably cheap at $9,500"* and added *"don't
think this will cost a tenth of what eScribe cost"* — Jeff laughed.

Intended pricing (not sure if all of this was communicated):
- $30 per meeting, ~$6,000 per year up-front for backfill
- Then $9,500 annually with no history

They said they'd send an email about the **unsolicited bid process**.
Paul asked whether that could get through purchasing — concern is that
purchasing could block it because they'd argue someone else could offer
it. Jeff said he'd look into it and include anything about that in the
follow-up email.

Paul is hoping this can go through purchasing no matter what.

## Adjacent thinking captured in the debrief

These came up in the post-call conversation rather than in the meeting
itself. Folded into [`docs/ROADMAP.md`](../docs/ROADMAP.md) and
[`docs/POSITIONING.md`](../docs/POSITIONING.md) where they belong.

- Self-host video cost ballpark (200 meetings/yr at 1080p, $12–30/mo
  storage; egress $0.005–0.01/GB; audio-only as fallback).
- Inference is the largest cost unknown. *"What do we do if demand is
  too high? The answer is: we're going to celebrate."*
- Up to ~20k Negation Games at full coverage (1,200 meetings × ~16 key
  topics each).
- Speaker name and title accuracy needs both online sources and an
  internal config layer.
- Competitors: eScribe sells to clerks; clerks aren't generally
  AI-buyers, and eScribe is unlikely to be good at it.
- Argument-maps whitepaper: history → why it didn't catch on
  (expressiveness vs. usability) → why AI changes it. Epistemic graphs
  as our subset. Possibility that these graphs are useful for
  constraining the AI to respond well.
