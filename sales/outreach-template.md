# Outreach email template

Locked template for Ontario municipal clerk cold outreach. Sent from
**Paul Winsor**. Used for the 42-clerk Coda batch (May 2026).

## Constraints

- No em dashes. Casual but institutional tone.
- Value prop framing: **search and understand how your council actually decides**.
- Calendar link: https://calendar.app.google/PeKe9ifG5jquHUia9
- Skip the demo link until the live site is up. Once
  `louie.networkgoods.institute` is live, the email can reference it.
- CASL-style opt-out is mandatory. Always include the line.
- One personalized paragraph per recipient, anchored to a real
  transparency policy or role-shape detail. Generic openings get filtered.

## Template

```
Subject: 2-min demo for [Town], worth a quick look?

Hi [Name],

I'm Paul Winsor, an Ontario resident who's spent the last 10+ years on
civic transparency.

Reaching out because my team built a tool that lets you search and
understand how your council actually decides. It turns the full record
(video, minutes, agendas, staff reports) into something you can query,
"when did this issue first come up, and who raised it?", and get an
answer in seconds, cited back to the exact transcript line.

[PERSONALIZED PARAGRAPH — anchor to a recent transparency policy, IC
report, code of conduct refresh, Strong Mayor Powers addition, new term
under Bill 9, or the shape of their role (combined CAO/Clerk, brand-new
appointee, deputy clerk catching reverse-lookup requests, etc.).]

Happy to send over the 2-min Mississauga demo, or grab a 20-min call,
whichever's less friction:
https://calendar.app.google/PeKe9ifG5jquHUia9

And if this isn't of interest and you'd like me to stop, just reply
and let me know.

Thanks either way,
Paul Winsor
```

## Personalization patterns that have worked

- **Combined CAO/Clerk role at a small town**: "you're carrying a lot in both
  roles, that probably means you *are* the institutional memory."
- **New clerk in seat (e.g. Saugeen Shores, Mar 2026)**: "Louie is essentially
  how a new clerk gets fluent in the last five years of council activity."
- **Deputy clerk**: "deputies usually feel the time savings first because you're
  the one fielding 'when was this raised?' questions."
- **Strong Mayor Powers expansion city**: "every mayoral direction has to be
  written and made public — that raises the stakes on residents being able
  to actually trace those decisions over time."
- **City under IC scrutiny / Ombudsman observation (e.g. Rideau Lakes)**:
  Tread carefully. Frame as a neutral-record aid, not piling on.
- **Upper-tier county (Perth, Prescott and Russell, etc.)**: "you've got
  multiple member municipalities all feeding into one County record" — the
  cross-record query angle is unique to upper-tier.
